# Day 11 Supplement: Decision-Making - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**What 80% of your interview impact comes from:**
1. **AML work is ambiguous** - rarely have perfect info
2. Use a **framework**, not gut feelings
3. **Prioritize** by risk (sanctions → protection → routine)
4. **Document** reasoning (for review later)

**The key insight:**
> Timely decisions beat perfect decisions. Waiting for perfect info = criminals winning.

**Framework:**
1. Risk score (1-10, gut)
2. Evidence check (red flags vs green flags)
3. Cost of error analysis
4. Bias check (am I being paranoid or trusting?)
5. Decide & document

---

## Systems Thinking Breakdown

```
ALERTS → TRIAGE → INVESTIGATION → DECISION → OUTPUT
  ↓         ↓           ↓         ↓
Flood    → Priority   Limited    Block/escalate/
in         Order       time        approve/monitor
```

**Triage priority:**
- 🔴 Sanctions: TODAY (non-negotiable)
- 🔴 Account compromise: TODAY (victim protection)
- 🟠 High $ + high risk: 24-48 hours
- 🟡 Baseline deviation: This week
- 🟢 Established + documented: Ongoing

---

## Interview Gold

**Q: "How do you make decisions with incomplete information?"**
> I use a framework. What's my confidence level? What's the downside if I'm wrong? Do I have time to wait for more info? In AML, waiting too long = money gone. I make the best call with what I have, document it, and escalate if needed.

**Q: "How do you prioritize when everything feels urgent?"**
> Not everything IS urgent. Sanctions violations are non-negotiable - must handle today. Customer account compromise (victim) - handle today. Everything else gets ranked. High amount + high risk patterns go before routine checks.

**Q: "What if you're wrong?"**
> If I block a legitimate customer - we unblock them once verified, apologize, move on. If I approve a criminal - that's a compliance failure. Generally, AML leans cautious but investigates first, doesn't blanket-block. Document reasoning = defensible.

---

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You have **a framework** (not random decisions)
- You **prioritize** systematically
- You're **confident** making calls
- You **document** your reasoning

**This shows they can trust your judgment.**
