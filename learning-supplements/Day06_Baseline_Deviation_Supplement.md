# Day 6 Supplement: Baseline Deviation - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**What 80% of your interview impact comes from:**
1. **Baseline = normal pattern** for that specific customer
2. **Deviation = change from normal**
3. **Key question:** Does this activity match their declared profile?
4. **Reassess against profile** = is it in-character or out-of-character?

**One-liner:**
"A baseline is what normal looks like for THIS customer. A deviation is anything that doesn't match. The key is context - a $10k transfer might be normal for a business, suspicious for a freelancer."

---

## Systems Thinking Breakdown

```
KYC DATA + HISTORY → BASELINE → NEW ACTIVITY → COMPARISON → OUTPUT
     ↓                 ↓          ↓            ↓
Declares:        Normal for   Activity   Match?    → Decision
Freelancer      $3k/month   $3k/month    ✅      → OK
$3k/month                  $30k          ❌      → Investigate
```

**The insight:**
- Same transaction = DIFFERENT risk depending on customer profile
- $50k from unknown source = suspicious for freelancer, normal for import business

**New vs established customers:**
- New (<30 days): Use profile as your "baseline"
- Established: Use 3-6 month history

---

## Interview Gold

**Q: "How do you detect baseline deviation?"**
> First, I know what's normal for this customer - their declared profile and history. Then I compare new transactions against that. A sudden spike in amount, new recipients, or high velocity from a customer who was previously slow = deviation.

**Q: "What's the difference between normal and suspicious deviation?"**
> Normal = explainable (seasonal, bonus, documented). Suspicious = contradicts profile, no explanation, higher risk indicators. Example: A freelancer gets 10x their normal amount from someone they've never mentioned = suspicious.

**Q: "Why does context matter?"**
> Because $50k is completely different for different customers. For a freelancer, it's unusual and needs verification. For an import business, it might be normal. Context = customer profile.

---

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You think in **context**, not absolutes
- You establish baselines before making judgments
- You can explain the difference between normal variation and suspicious deviation
- You use the customer's profile as your reference point

**Red flag answer:** "I flag anything over $10k" → Too simplistic, no thinking.

---

## Quick Reference: Risk Context

| Situation | Why It's OK | Why It's Suspicious |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Normal amount spike | December/holiday bonus | Unknown source, new recipients |
| New vendor | Provided documentation | Can't verify, no docs |
| High velocity | Explained by business | No explanation, repeated |

**The key question:** Does the activity MATCH what they said they'd do?
