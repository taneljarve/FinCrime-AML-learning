# Day 9 Supplement: Shell Companies - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**What 80% of your interview impact comes from:**
1. **Shell company = paper company, no real operations**
2. **Nominee directors** = professional figureheads (director of 47 companies = red flag)
3. **Beneficial ownership** = who REALLY controls the company
4. **Golden Source check** = verify against official business registry

**One-liner:**
"Shell companies exist on paper but have no real operations. They're used to hide ownership. I verify using official business registries - if a company has a nominee director and no real address, that's a shell."

**Why shells are used:**

- Look like legitimate businesses (invoices, registered)
- Hide ultimate beneficial owner
- Your job: Remove layers to find real owner

---

## Interview Gold

**Q: "How do you investigate a shell company?"**
> First, check the official business registry - is it actually registered? Second, look up directors - if someone has 47 companies, they're a professional nominee. Third, check for real operations - address, website, financials. Fourth, request ownership documentation.

**Q: "What's a nominee director?"**
> Someone who lends their name to hundreds of companies. If one person is director of 50+ companies they can't possibly manage, they're a professional nominee - hiding the real owner. That's a massive red flag.

**Q: "Why does beneficial ownership matter?"**
> Because the person who BENEFICIALLY owns the money is who matters legally. Shell companies hide this person. I need to trace beyond nominee directors to find who actually controls the company.
