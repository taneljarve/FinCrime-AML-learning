# Day 2 Supplement: KYC & Verification - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**What 80% of your interview impact comes from:**
1. **KYC = baseline** - Without it, you can't spot deviations
2. **Golden Source Rule** - Always verify against official registers, not company websites
3. **CDD vs EDD** - Basic vs enhanced for high-risk customers
4. **The question:** "Does their activity match what they declared?"

**One-liner:**
"KYC builds a customer profile so you know what's normal for THEM. Then you spot deviations. The Golden Source Rule means you verify claims against official government databases, not what the customer tells you."

## Interview Gold

**Q: "Why is KYC important if we have transaction monitoring?"**
> Transaction monitoring only works IF you know what's normal for that customer. Without KYC baseline, you can't tell if $50k is suspicious or just a normal large invoice for a business customer. KYC IS the foundation.

**Q: "What's the Golden Source Rule?"**
> Verify claims against official government databases (FCA register, Companies House, SEC EDGAR), NOT company websites or LinkedIn. Those are self-reported and can be faked.

**Q: "When do you trigger Enhanced Due Diligence?"**
> PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, unusual volume, activity that doesn't match declared purpose - basically anything that raises the risk profile from initial onboarding.

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You understand KYC as **foundation**, not paperwork
- You know how to verify independently (Golden Source)
- You can spot mismatches between declared profile and activity
- You ask "Does this make sense for this customer?"

**Real talk:** Interviewers want to see you think critically about data, not just check boxes.

## Quick Reference: KYC Red Flags

| Flag | Why It Matters |
|------|---------------|
| Source of funds doesn't match activity | Could be hidden dirty money |
| New country with no history | Needs verification |
| PEP status | Higher risk threshold |
| Inconsistent documents | Identity risk |
| Can't verify declared business | Shell company risk |
