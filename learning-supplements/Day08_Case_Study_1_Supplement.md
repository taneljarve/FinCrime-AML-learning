# Day 8 Supplement: Case Study 1 - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**What 80% of your interview impact comes from:**
1. Real cases have **conflicting signals** (some good, some bad)
2. **Weigh evidence** before deciding
3. Most defensible call: **Escalate** (don't block prematurely, don't approve blindly)
4. **Document reasoning** so others can understand

**The key skill:**
Balancing multiple factors. April-May was legitimate. June showed red flags. The right call is investigate, not block or approve.

---

## Systems Thinking Breakdown

```
CASE INPUT → EVIDENCE WEIGHING → DECISION → OUTPUT
   ↓              ↓            ↓
Mix of         → Both good   → Investigate
Red/Green        + bad        → vs Block
                                vs Approve
```

**Decision framework:**
1. What's the primary risk? (Most likely fraud or legit?)
2. Check most important factors (invoices, matching, plausibility)
3. Cost of error (false positive vs false negative)
4. Make the call
5. Document clearly

---

## Interview Gold

**Q: "Walk me through your decision on FastTech."**
> Looking at the case: April-May showed legitimate growth - matching invoices, business logic, gradual increase. June showed red flags - new source, personal accounts, same-day velocity, missing docs. The most defensible call is escalate for enhanced due diligence. We need more information before blocking a potentially legitimate business.

**Q: "Why not just block immediately?"**
> Because April-May were legitimate. Blocking a good customer hurts Wise's reputation and could be wrong. Need to give them chance to explain. But June activity is too suspicious to ignore - escalate for verification.

**Q: "What would change your mind?"**
> Documentation showing CloudSync is a real company with real operations. Explanation for personal account transfers that makes business sense. Without that, it stays escalated.

---

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You can **weigh conflicting evidence**
- You make **defensible decisions**
- You're not reckless but not paranoid either
- You document your reasoning

**The wisdom:** Interviewers want someone who investigates BEFORE escalating, not someone who escalates everything.

---

## Quick Framework

**When you see mixed signals:**

1. **Primary assessment:** More red flags or green flags?
2. **Most important factors:** Invoices present? Business logic? Plausible?
3. **Cost of error:** If I block a good customer vs miss a bad one
4. **Decision:**
   - More green flags → Monitor
   - More red flags → Escalate EDD
   - Clear fraud → Block

5. **Document** your reasoning!
