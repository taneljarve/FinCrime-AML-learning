# Day 4 Supplement: Red Flags - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**The 4 Core Red Flags (memorize these):**

| Red Flag | Definition | Interview Answer |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **Velocity** | Money moves fast through account | "High velocity = money in and out same day - classic layering pattern" |
| **Retention** | Funds don't stay | "< 24 hour retention = account as conduit" |
| **Recipient Variety** | Many unrelated recipients | "One-in-many-out = distributing to hide source" |
| **Documentation** | Missing or mismatched | "No invoice = no verification = red flag" |

**One-liner:**
"Red flags are patterns that don't match the customer's declared profile. High velocity (fast in/fast out), low retention (money doesn't stay), many unrelated recipients, and missing documentation are the big four."

## Systems Thinking Breakdown

```
NORMAL FLOW → RED FLAG PATTERN → INVESTIGATION → OUTPUT
   ↓            ↓                ↓
Funds stay   → Suspicious    → Escalate/
in account      Pattern      Block
(1-2 weeks)    Detected       → 
```

**The system insight:**
- Red flags aren't proof of crime - they're **reason to investigate**
- Multiple flags + no explanation = higher confidence
- Single flag might be legitimate (seasonal, bonus, etc.)

**Feedback loop:**
- You escalate → Compliance reviews → Decision → Feedback improves your flagging accuracy

## Interview Gold

**Q: "What's your first check when reviewing an account?"**
> I establish the baseline: What's normal for THIS customer? Then I look for deviations - velocity, retention, recipients, documentation.

**Q: "Give me an example of a red flag."**
> Customer declares "freelancer, $3k/month" but suddenly receives $30k and sends it to 7 personal accounts same day. That doesn't match their profile. High velocity + recipient variety = suspicious.

**Q: "What's a pass-through account?"**
> An account receiving money from one source and immediately sending to multiple other accounts - like a funnel. Classic layering. Money comes in clean, goes out dirty.

---

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You use a **framework** (not gut feelings)
- You can explain red flags **simply**
- You understand context matters (customer profile vs activity)
- You're methodical, not paranoid

**Good vs Bad red flag thinking:**
- ❌ "Large transaction = suspicious" (too simple)
- ✅ "Large transaction from NEW SOURCE that doesn't match declared business = needs verification" (contextual)

## Quick Pattern Guide

**🔴 HIGH RISK (escalate today):**
- Same-day in/out cycles
- 5+ recipient accounts
- Missing documentation
- Sanctioned country

**🟡 MEDIUM RISK (investigate this week):**
- Volume increase explained by business
- New vendor with documentation
- One-time large payment with invoice

**🟢 LOW RISK (ongoing monitoring):**
- Consistent with profile
- Documented business purpose
- No high-risk indicators
