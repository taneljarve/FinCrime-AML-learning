# Interview 80/20 - Must-Know Q&A

## The Questions That Matter Most (20% effort, 80% impact)

### Day 1-3: Fundamentals

**Q1: Walk me through what placement, layering, and integration mean.**
> **Placement (Stage 1):** Getting illegal cash into the financial system - criminals deposit money, claim it's business revenue. Problem: Large deposits get flagged.
> **Layering (Stage 2):** Moving money through complex transactions to hide the source. Multiple transfers, shell companies, different countries. This is where YOU work - catching it in transaction monitoring.
> **Integration (Stage 3):** Making the money look legitimate. Fake business revenue, real estate, appears as normal income. Hardest to catch.

**Q2: Why does Wise care about AML?**
> Wise is a licensed fintech with legal obligations. Regulatory law requires AML compliance. Failure = fines up to 10% of global revenue + reputation damage + customer safety risk.

**Q3: What is KYC?**
> Know Your Customer - obtaining and verifying info about customers before/onboarding. Establishes baseline so you can spot deviations.

**Q4: What is the Golden Source Rule?**
> Always verify claims against official government registers, NOT company websites/LinkedIn. Official sources = harder to forge, legally binding.

### Day 4-7: Red Flags

**Q5: What are the 4 core red flags?**
> 1. **Velocity** - money in/out fast (same day = suspicious)
> 2. **Retention** - funds don't stay (<24 hours = account as conduit)
> 3. **Recipient Variety** - sending to many unrelated accounts
> 4. **Documentation** - missing invoices, amounts don't match

**Q6: What's high velocity and why is it suspicious?**
> Same-day in/out cycles. Classic layering - account used to hide money source, not for real business.

**Q7: What's a pass-through account?**
> Account receiving funds from one source and immediately distributing to multiple unrelated accounts. Funnel pattern = layering.

**Q8: What makes baseline deviation suspicious vs. normal?**
> Suspicious: Contradicts customer profile, unexplained, new high-risk channels. Normal: Explained by business logic (seasonal, bonus), matches profile.

### Day 8-10: Case Studies

**Q9: What's the difference between account misuse and account owner fraud?**
> Account Misuse: Legitimate account, compromised by criminal (customer is VICTIM). Your job: Protect customer.
> Account Owner Fraud: Customer is the fraudster. Your job: Block and escalate.

**Q10: Give an example of when AML was about protecting customers rather than catching criminals.**
> Elderly customer with account compromise - suspicious activity turned out to be stolen credentials. Froze account, helped secure it, recovered funds = protection, not just blocking.

**Q11: How do you investigate a shell company?**
> 1. Check official business registry - real?
> 2. Check financials - real operations or empty?
> 3. Analyze directors - nominee (40+ companies)?
> 4. Check OFAC/sanctions
> 5. Call company - do they answer?
> 6. Look for patterns - same address as other shells?

### Day 11-15: Interview Prep

**Q12: How do you make decisions with incomplete information?**
> Use a framework: 1) What's my confidence level? 2) What's downside if wrong? 3) Do I have time to wait? Timely > perfect. Document reasoning.

**Q13: What would you do if a customer refused to provide documentation?**
> Low risk: Ask again politely. High risk: "We need this to process. Without it, we can't approve." Set deadline. If refuse + high risk signals: Block. Refusal itself is a red flag.

**Q14: What's your biggest strength for this role?**
> Pattern recognition + attention to detail + calm under pressure. From trading, I'm trained to spot anomalies in data and make decisions with incomplete info.

**Q15: Why do you want to work at Wise?**
> Financial access is a right, not a privilege. Wise's mission to make global money transfer fair resonates. Your value "Customers > team > ego" aligns with my belief that compliance protects customers, not just blocks transactions.

## The Mistakes That Lose Interviews

**❌ "I just flag transactions and move on"**
> Sounds passive. Shows no ownership.

**❌ "I'd block everything over $10k"**
> Too simplistic. No thinking.

**❌ "I don't know" (without next step)**
> You can say "I don't know, BUT I'd approach it by..." Show framework thinking.

**❌ Can't explain AML basics**
> If you can't explain placement/layering/integration simply, you haven't learned enough.

**❌ Generic "Why Wise?"**
> "Seems like a cool company" = instant red flag. Know the mission.

**❌ Badmouthing previous job/team**
> Shows unprofessionalism. Even if true, don't.
