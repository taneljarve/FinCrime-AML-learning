## Systems Thinking Breakdown

**The AML System as a Whole:**
```
INPUTS → PROCESS → OUTPUTS
Illegal $ → Detection → Clean/Frozen
  ↓         ↓
Rules    → Your Investigation → Decision
& Tech     + Escalation
```

**Feedback Loops:**
- Criminals adapt to detection → you must evolve patterns
- New regulations ← enforcement failures
- Your investigation → improves detection rules (for everyone)

**Leverage Points (where you create most impact):**
1. **Transaction monitoring** (catches layering - your main job)
2. **KYC verification** (prevents bad actors at entry)
3. **Escalation quality** (improves整个 system decisions)

**System Failure Modes:**
- False positives → blocking legitimate customers → reputation damage
- False negatives → missing criminals → regulatory fines + license risk
- Your job: Balance both

## Interview Gold

**Q: "Why does money laundering happen?"**
> Criminals need to use their money without revealing it came from crime. If they spend dirty money directly, it gets traced back to the crime.

**Q: "Which stage is hardest to detect?"**
> Layering. It's designed to hide the trail through multiple transactions. Placement has obvious signs (cash deposits). Integration looks legitimate. Layering is where YOUR investigation matters most.

**Q: "How does your trading background help in AML?"**
> Pattern recognition, risk management under uncertainty, staying calm when data is incomplete - all transferable.

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You understand AML is about **protecting customers** not just blocking transactions
- You know the **three stages** and can explain them simply
- You connect Wise's mission to compliance work
- You take **ownership** ("I spot patterns, I investigate, I document")

**Red flag answer:** "I just flag suspicious transactions and move on." → Sounds passive, not owner mindset.
