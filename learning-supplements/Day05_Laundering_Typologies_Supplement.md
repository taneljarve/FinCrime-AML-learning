# Day 5 Supplement: Typologies - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**What 80% of your interview impact comes from:**

| Stage | Definition | Your Job Focus |
|-------|------------|----------------|
| **Placement** | Money enters system | KYC stops it here |
| **Layering** | Hides source | YOU monitor here |
| **Integration** | Reappears as clean | Hardest to catch |

**Why layering is YOUR job:**
- Placement = cash deposits (easy to flag)
- Integration = assets (outside your system)
- Layering = transaction patterns (happens ON your platform, visible to you)

**One-liner:**
"Placement gets dirty money in. Layering hides where it came from through multiple transfers. Integration cleans it through fake businesses or assets. My job catches layering because that's where the traffic flows through financial platforms."

---

## Systems Thinking Breakdown

```
CRIMINAL → PLACEMENT → LAYERING → INTEGRATION → CLEAN MONEY
GOAL:    Input dirty   Hide trail   Make legit    Use freely
  ↓          ↓           ↓
Detected by: KYC      YOU!         External
at:        (onboard)   (monitor)    (harder)
```

**Typology patterns to know:**
- **Structuring:** Many deposits just below $10k to avoid reporting
- **Smurfing:** Multiple small transactions, same person
- **Shell companies:** Fake businesses with no real operations
- **Trade-based:** Over/under-invoicing imports/exports

---

## Interview Gold

**Q: "Walk me through the three stages."**
> Placement is getting money into the system - cash deposits, converting to bank funds. Layering is moving it through multiple accounts to hide the source - that's where I do my work. Integration is making it look like legitimate income through fake businesses or assets.

**Q: "Why is layering your focus?"**
> Because I can see it happening on Wise's platform. Cash placements are easier to flag at onboarding. Integration is when the money leaves as "clean" - much harder to detect. Layering happens in real-time, visible in transaction patterns.

**Q: "What's a common laundering method?"**
> Shell companies are huge - fake businesses created just to move money. Look for brand new companies with no real operations, nominee directors (people who are directors of 50+ companies), virtual addresses, no financials filed.

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You know the **three stages** and can explain them
- You understand **where your job fits** in the system
- You know common typologies (structuring, shell companies)
- You're curious about evolving patterns

**Expert mention:** If you can reference a real case (Danske Bank, FinCEN sanctions on crypto), it shows depth.
