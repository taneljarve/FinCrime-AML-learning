# Day 10 Supplement: Victim Protection - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**Critical insight:**
> Not ALL suspicious activity = customer is the criminal

**Sometimes the CUSTOMER IS THE VICTIM:**
- Account takeover (stolen credentials)
- Elder financial abuse
- Romance/investment scams

**Your job includes BOTH:**
- Protecting Wise from criminals
- Protecting customers from fraudsters

**One-liner:**
"Sometimes accounts show suspicious activity because the customer is a victim, not a criminal. Account takeover fraud is when criminals steal login details and drain accounts. With elderly customers, especially - check if they're victim first."

## Systems Thinking Breakdown

```
USUAL FLOW                   VICTIM FLOW
Criminal → Money out         Victim ← Criminal access
   ↓                           ↓
YOU block                    YOU protect
fraudster                   customer

Both matter: Catching crime AND preventing victim harm
```

**Why this distinction matters:**
- Different actions (block vs freeze + help)
- Different tone (accusatory vs supportive)
- Legal + reputational implications

---

## Interview Gold

**Q: "Give an example of when AML was about protecting customers."**
> That accounts case where an elderly customer suddenly showed weird activity. Instead of blocking and accusing, we called and found out they'd been compromised. Froze the account, helped them secure it, and recovered what we could. That was about protection.

**Q: "How do you distinguish victim from fraud?"**
> Look at the pattern. Victims: sudden change from normal, deny knowledge, age/profile fits scam target, no motive to launder. Fraudsters: have motive, hide activity, evade questions. Also, elderly + recent life events = vulnerability indicators.

**Q: "What would you do if an elderly customer's account shows suspicious activity?"**
> I wouldn't block immediately - I'd call. There's a real chance they're a victim. Check: Do they know about this activity? Can they explain? If they genuinely don't know - that's account compromise. Protect them, don't just block them.

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You understand **both sides** of the job
- You can protect **without assuming guilt**
- You have empathy for **vulnerable customers**
- You balance compliance WITH customer experience

**This shows Wise values:** "Customers > team > ego" - protect the customer genuinely.

## Quick Victim Checklist

When to suspect VICTIM (not fraud):
- [ ] Elderly customer (65+)
- [ ] Sudden activity change after dormancy
- [ ] Denies knowledge when asked
- [ ] No motive to launder money
- [ ] Activity doesn't match their lifestyle
- [ ] Recent life events (grief, retirement)
