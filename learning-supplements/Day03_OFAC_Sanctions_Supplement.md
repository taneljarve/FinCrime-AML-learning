# Day 3 Supplement: OFAC Sanctions - Interview Accelerator

## 80/20 Summary

**What 80% of your interview impact comes from:**
1. **OFAC = US Treasury sanctions** - can't be ignored, huge fines
2. **SDN List** - Specially Designated Nationals (the main list)
3. **High-risk jurisdictions** = grey/black list countries (FATF)
4. **Any sanctions hit = block immediately** (non-negotiable)

**One-liner:**
"OFAC is US Treasury's sanctions list. Any transfer to a sanctioned person/country MUST be blocked. There's no judgment call - it's the law. Wise uses automated screening but you're the human check."

## Systems Thinking Breakdown

```
SANCTIONS INPUT → AUTOMATED SCREEN → MANUAL REVIEW → OUTPUT
  ↓
Customer      → Flag Matches  → Verify Match  → Block/Allow
Name/Address                       → Escalate
```

**Critical system rule:**
- **Zero tolerance** for sanctions violations
- One miss = $1B+ fine potential (bankruptcy-level risk)
- Your job: Verify automated flags aren't false positives

**Why sanctions exist in the system:**
- Counterterrorism financing
- Rogue nation restrictions
- Human rights enforcement
- Non-negotiable legal requirement

**Leverage point:** Your manual verification prevents:
- False positives (blocking legitimate customers)
- False negatives (missing real hits - catastrophic)

---

## Interview Gold

**Q: "What happens if you find a real sanctions match?"**
> Block immediately. Then escalate to compliance/legal. File a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) if required. Document everything. No exceptions.

**Q: "What's the difference between a sanctions hit and false positive?"**
> A hit is an exact match on name/DOB/details. A false positive is a name that just sounds similar (common name, different person). That's why your manual verification matters.

**Q: "Why is this a top priority at Wise?"**
> Wise moves money internationally. Cross-border transfers are higher risk for sanctions evasion. One miss = massive fine + potentially losing US license.

---

## What Interviewers Want to Hear

- You understand sanctions are **non-negotiable** (not discretion)
- You know the screening process (automated + manual layers)
- You can handle a hit professionally (block, don't panic)
- You're meticulous with verification

**Red flag answer:** "I'd probably let it through if it looked small" → Instant disqualification. Zero tolerance.
