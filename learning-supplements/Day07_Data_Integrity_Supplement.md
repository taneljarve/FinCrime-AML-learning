## Interview Gold

**Q: "How do you stay accurate with large volumes?"**
> I use a systematic checklist. For every case, I verify: names (spelling), dates (timeline logic), amounts (totals match), documentation (all backed), jurisdiction (high-risk flags), business logic (matches profile). I don't rush - accuracy is the job.

**Q: "What's a data entry error vs intentional obfuscation?"**
> Data entry error = single typo, obvious reversal, no pattern. Intentional obfuscation = multiple inconsistencies, strategic changes, makes tracing harder. If I see many mismatches across different fields, that's suspicious.

**Q: "Give me an example of catching an error."**
> Analyst notes say "$3,000 total received" but table shows $3,200. That's $200 discrepancy - could be structuring. I catch it by comparing notes to data, not trusting either automatically.
