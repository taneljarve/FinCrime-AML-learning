## Key Concepts  

### The STAR Method

Wise interviews will ask behavioral questions: "Tell me about a time when..."

**Format: SITUATION → TASK → ACTION → RESULT**

**S - SITUATION:**  
Set the scene (2-3 sentences). Who, what, where, when context.

**T - TASK:**  
What was the specific challenge/goal? What did you need to achieve?

**A - ACTION:**  
What did YOU do? (Focus on your individual contribution, not team.)

**R - RESULT:**  
What was the outcome? Quantify if possible. Were there learnings?

**Example:**
```
Situation: "At my previous job, I was processing 200 loan applications per week."
Task: "I noticed we were missing due dates on 5-10% of applications due to data entry errors."
Action: "I created a checklist system and implemented a peer-review process for high-risk applications."
Result: "Error rate dropped to <1%. Team's approval rate increased 15%. I was asked to train new hires on the system."
```

### What Wise Looks For in Answers

**1. Ownership & Initiative**
- Do you OWN problems or blame others?
- Do you see an issue and ACT?  
- Wise value: "We get it done"

**2. Customer Focus**
- Do you think about impact on users?
- Do you prioritize customer outcomes?
- Wise value: "Customers > team > ego"

**3. Teamwork & Communication**
- Do you collaborate or work in silos?
- Can you explain complex ideas simply?
- Wise value: "No drama. Good karma"

**4. Learning from Failure**
- Can you talk honestly about mistakes?
- Do you reflect and improve?
- Wise value: "Sometimes we fail, and that's OK"

**5. Decision-Making Under Pressure**
- Can you make calls with incomplete info?  
- Do you stay calm under stress?
- How do you prioritize competing demands?

### TODO 1: Build Your STAR Story on Attention to Detail

**What to do:**  
Think of a real example from your life (job, school, side project) where you:
- Caught a mistake or inconsistency
- That others missed
- That had impact (prevented a problem, improved something)

Using STAR format, write your story:

```
SITUATION:
[2-3 sentences: Where were you? What was happening?]

TASK:
[1-2 sentences: What was the challenge or goal?]

ACTION:
[3-4 sentences: What did YOU do specifically?]

RESULT:
[2-3 sentences: What happened? What did you learn?]
```

**Example Framework:**
- Situation: "I was reviewing transaction data from 50 customers..."
- Task: "I needed to spot inconsistencies between reports and transaction records..."
- Action: "I created a check list and found that 2 customers had mismatched amounts..."
- Result: "Reported to my manager, we prevented potential fraud, I was asked to audit other high-risk accounts..."

**Why:**  
Day 12 of the interview will include "Tell me about a time you spotted something important." This story is your answer.

**Validation:**  
Your story is real (not made up), shows YOU (not team), has a clear problem + solution, and demonstrates Wise value of "getting it done."

**SITUATION:** I was reviewing a routine account with 6 months of normal activity (freelancer, regular invoices, consistent vendors).

**TASK:** I noticed the customer's April-May activity was normal, but in June received a $35k transfer from a new source ("CloudSync LLC") and immediately distributed $30k to 5 personal accounts (never done before). This pattern completely contradicted her baseline.

### TODO 2: Build Your STAR Story on Teamwork Under Pressure

**What to do:**  
Think of a time when you:
- Faced pressure (deadline, high stakes, multiple demands)
- Had to work WITH others (or manage conflict)
- Stayed professional and got results

Write your STAR story:

```
SITUATION:
[Set the scene]

TASK:
[What was pressuring you?]

ACTION:
[What did YOU do? How did you handle pressure?]

RESULT:
[Outcome + what you learned]
```

**Example:**
- Situation: "My team had to process 1,000 transactions by end of day for a client review."
- Task: "It was our first huge batch. People were stressed. Two team members disagreed on process."
- Action: "I suggested we split the batch, each person own a section, and held brief sync meetings to align. I stayed calm and asked clarifying questions instead of arguing."
- Result: "We finished on time with 99.2% accuracy. Team told me they appreciated how I kept morale up."

**Why:**  
Wise will ask: "How do you handle pressure?" This story shows you can stay calm + collaborate.

**Validation:**  
Your story shows: (1) You stayed professional, (2) You communicated well, (3) You got results, (4) You reflect on what went well.

<details>
<summary>💡 Example Story (Click to reveal)</summary>

**SITUATION:** Our compliance team had to review 500 flagged transactions submitted by automated systems before month-end deadline (5pm today). We were short one analyst due to illness.

**TASK:** Pressure was intense—500 transactions, 3 people normally, 2 people today, each transaction needing 10-15 min investigation. My colleague Sarah was frustrated ("this can't be done"), another colleague wanted to rush through ("flag everything as suspicious"). Everyone was stressed.

**ACTION:** First, I stayed calm and said, "Let's prioritize, not panic." I suggested we (1) Sort transactions by risk: sanctions first (mandatory), then high-amount, then routine, (2) Divide the work: I took high-risk (most complex), Sarah took medium, colleague took routine, (3) Set milestones: 100 per hour, brief sync every 60 min to flag blockers. I also made coffee and said, "We've handled worse. Let's focus on quality, not speed." When Sarah hit a complex shell company case, I helped her work through Golden Source Rule verification without making her feel bad for asking.

**RESULT:** We reviewed all 500 by 4:45pm with 0 errors (checked each other's work). Sarah later told me she appreciated how I didn't let panic take over. Manager realized this was efficient teamwork and adopted our prioritization strategy for future high-volume periods.

**Why This Works:** Shows leadership (took charge without being bossy), collaboration (helped teammates), professionalism (stayed calm), AND got results (met deadline with quality).

</details>
