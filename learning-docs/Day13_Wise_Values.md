## Key Concepts

### Wise's 4 Core Values

From https://wise.jobs/our-values:

#### 1. **"This isn't just a job, we're a revolution"**

**What It Means:**  
Wise isn't a normal fintech. We're changing finance globally for real people.

**The Expectation:**
- You care about the MISSION (fair global money transfer), not just a paycheck
- You're inspired by impact
- You want to be part of something bigger
- You can articulate WHY financial inclusion matters

**In FinCrime Role:**  
"Our AML work protects customers from criminal exploitation. That's part of the revolution."

**Red Flag to Avoid:**  
"I just need a job" or "I'm looking for high salary" → Wrong mindset for Wise.

#### 2. **"We get it done"**

**What It Means:**  
We own problems. We take action. We don't wait for permission.

**The Expectation:**
- When you see a problem, you SOLVE it (or escalate smartly)
- You take ownership ("This is mine")
- You break through obstacles
- You deliver results

**In FinCrime Role:**  
"I see suspicious activity, I investigate immediately. I don't delay. I document and escalate as needed."

**Red Flag to Avoid:**  
"I waited for guidance" or "That wasn't my responsibility" → Wise wants owners, not robots.

#### 3. **"Customers > team > ego"**

**What It Means:**  
Customer outcomes come first. Your team comes second. Your ego comes third.

**The Expectation:**
- You protect customer interests above all
- You're humble (no ego)
- You support teammates  
- You challenge ideas respectfully, not people

**In FinCrime Role:**  
"When I investigate accounts, I genuinely protect customers from fraud. I'm humble enough to ask for help. I don't blame colleagues."

**Red Flag to Avoid:**  
"I disagreed so I was right" or "I always put myself first" → Wise values collective mission over individual achievement.

#### 4. **"No drama. Good karma"**

**What It Means:**  
Professional, respectful communication. Assume good intentions. Be honest and transparent.

**The Expectation:**
- You communicate clearly and directly
- You give others the benefit of doubt
- You don't gossip or create conflict
- You're open about information (no hidden agendas)

**In FinCrime Role:**  
"When I flag a customer, I explain my reasoning clearly. I don't assume guilt; I gather evidence. If a colleague disagrees, I discuss respectfully."

**Red Flag to Avoid:**  
"People were being toxic" or "I didn't trust them" → Wise assumes good faith and professional communication.

## How Wise Assesses Culture Fit

### Questions You Might Get

**1. "Tell me about a time you disagreed with someone at work. How did you handle it?"**
- Answer shows: Respect, communication, ego-checking
- Wise wants: "I listened. We discussed respectfully. We found common ground."
- Not: "I was right. They were wrong. I went over their head."

**2. "Describe your approach to solving a complex problem at work."**
- Answer shows: Ownership, action-orientation, collaboration
- Wise wants: "I owned it. I gathered info. I asked for input. I solved it."
- Not: "I waited for my manager to tell me what to do."

**3. "Why do you want to work at Wise specifically (not just any fintech)?"**
- Answer shows: Mission-alignment, awareness of Wise values
- Wise wants: "This mission excites me. Your values align with mine."
- Not: "You pay well" or "You're just another fintech."

**4. "Tell me about a time you had to admit you were wrong."**
- Answer shows: Humility, learning
- Wise wants: "I made a mistake. I owned it. I learned."
- Not: "I've never been wrong" or "It wasn't really my fault."

### The Red Flag Questions

Wise also looks for RED FLAGS. Answers that show:
- ❌ Ego-driven (bragging, taking credit)
- ❌ Disrespect for colleagues
- ❌ Not customer-focused
- ❌ Unwilling to learn
- ❌ Blame others for failures

**Why:**  
During the interview, they'll ask about values. If you can instantly point to examples, you show authentic alignment.

**Hint:**  
Use your STAR stories from Day 12 if they fit. Otherwise, use work, school, volunteer, personal examples. Authenticity matters most.

### TODO 2: Prepare Your "Why Wise?" Speech (Extended)

**What to do:**  
Build on Day 12's answer. Prepare a 60-second speech:

```
"I want to work at Wise because... [Mission]

Specifically, I align with the value '[Which value]' because [personal story].

In the FinCrime role, I'll embody this by [specific action you'll take].

```

**Example:**
```
"I want to work at Wise because I believe everyone deserves affordable, fast access to global money transfer. That's revolutionary.

In the FinCrime role, I'll protect customers by being rigorous with investigations and humble enough to ask colleagues when I'm unsure.

And I'm excited to join a team that values getting stuff done while keeping customers first. That's where I want to contribute and grow."
```

**Why:**  
You want to sound natural and authentic, not robotic. Practice this out loud until it flows.

**Validation:**  
Your speech: mentions mission, shows specific value alignment with example, connects to role, sounds authentic.

**Hint:**  
Practice saying it out loud WITH EMOTION. Let your genuine excitement for Wise come through.

### TODO 3: Red Flag Self-Check

**What to do:**  
Before the interview, check yourself against these RED FLAGS. Be honest:

```
RED FLAG CHECKLIST - Am I showing these in my interview prep?

[ ] I keep saying "I" instead of "we" (balanced ownership vs. teamwork)
[ ] I bad-mouth previous colleagues or managers
[ ] I focus only on pay/benefits, not mission
[ ] I can't articulate Wise's values clearly
[ ] I make excuses instead of owning mistakes
[ ] I interrupt or dismissive others' ideas
[ ] I don't ask questions (not curious)
[ ] I don't smile or seem excited about Wise
```

**For any checked boxes:**
Write ONE WAY you'll fix it before the interview.

**Why:**  
Self-awareness = interview confidence. You'll catch your own potential red flags.

**Validation:**  
Fewer than 2 red flags = you're well-prepared. If 3+, practice more.

**Hint:**  
The biggest red flag: Not being authentically excited about Wise. If you're not genuinely fired up about their mission, interviewers will sense it.
