## Key Concepts

### What is Money Laundering?

**Simple Definition:**  
Money laundering is the process of making **illegal money look legal** so it can be used freely without detection.

**The 3 Stages:**

1. **Placement** — Getting dirty money into the financial system
   - Example: Converting cash from drug dealings into a bank deposit
   - Challenge: Large cash deposits raise suspicion

2. **Layering** — Moving money through multiple transactions to hide its source
   - Example: Transferring $50k from Account A → Account B → Account C → Personal account
   - Challenge: Hide the trail between dirty source and clean destination
   - Your job: Spot these "pass-through" patterns

3. **Integration** — Reintroducing the laundered money as legitimate income
   - Example: Fake business generates "revenue" that is now "clean"
   - Challenge: Make it look normal

**Why This Matters:**  
Criminals use money laundering to hide proceeds from:
- Drug trafficking
- Human trafficking
- Corruption
- Terrorist financing
- Fraud
- Tax evasion

### Why Does Wise Care About AML?

Wise is a **licensed financial institution**. That means:

✅ **Legal Obligation** — Regulatory law requires AML compliance (FATF, EU directives, local laws)  
✅ **Reputation Risk** — One failure could destroy customer trust and license  
✅ **Financial Risk** — Fines: up to 10% of global revenue for compliance failures  
✅ **Customer Safety** — Protecting our users from financial crime  

**Wise's Values Connection:**  
- "Customers > team > ego" = Our customers deserve a safe platform
- "We get it done" = We own compliance; it's not just Legal's job
- "No drama. Good karma" = We stop bad actors proactively

### Your Role: FinCrime Operations Investigator

**What You Do:**
- 🔍 **Monitor** customer transactions for suspicious patterns
- 📋 **Analyze** transaction history against customer profile (KYC data)
- 🚨 **Identify** red flags and escalate for investigation
- 📝 **Document** findings clearly and thoroughly
- 🤝 **Collaborate** with team leads and other investigators

**Why It Matters:**  
You are the **first line of defense** between criminals and financial abuse.

### Answer 1: The 3 Stages
**Placement** (getting money into the system), **Layering** (hiding the source with multiple transactions), **Integration** (reintroducing as legitimate income)

### Answer 2: Why Money Laundering Happens
Criminals need to hide proceeds from illegal activities to use the money without detection. Direct use would reveal the crime.

As a FinCrime Investigator, you identify suspicious patterns (red flags) in transactions that might indicate money laundering. You escalate cases for investigation and help prevent financial crime. You're the first barrier.

### Answer 4: Hardest Stage
**Layering** is hardest to detect because it's designed to obscure the connection between source and destination. It uses complexity and velocity to hide the pattern. This is where most investigation effort goes.
