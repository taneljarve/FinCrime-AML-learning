## Key Concepts

### What is the Live Red Flag Assessment?

**The Interview Schedule:**
- 0:00-15 min: Introduction + chat about home assignment
- 15-~35 min: Behavioral questions (STAR stories)
- 35-50 min: **LIVE PAYMENT PATTERN REVIEW** ← The hard part
- 50-60 min: Your questions + closing

**The Live Assessment Format:**
1. They show you a payment pattern (transaction history + KYC data) for a customer for 3-5 minutes
2. You review quickly
3. They ask: "What red flags do you spot? What's suspicious?"
4. You respond with observations (they DON'T tell you if you're "right" - they're assessing your thinking)
5. Follow-up questions: "What would you do next? How would you prioritize?"

**What They're Assessing:**
- ✅ Can you spot red flags quickly?
- ✅ Can you articulate observations clearly?
- ✅ Do you use framework/logic or just guess?
- ✅ Do you think like an investigator?
- ✅ Can you stay calm under time pressure?

### Core Red Flags to Recognize (Review)

| Flag | What to Look For |
|------|-----------------|
| **Velocity** | Money in & out same day repeatedly |
| **Retention** | Funds leave account in <24 hours |
| **Recipient Variety** | Many unrelated accounts (especially personal) |
| **Documentation** | Missing invoices or amounts don't match |
| **High-Risk Country** | Transfers to sanctioned/high-risk areas |
| **Unknown Source** | Funds from entities you can't verify |
| **Pass-Through** | Money comes in, splits immediately to many accounts |
| **Profile Deviation** | Activity contradicts customer's declared purpose |
| **Age + Profile** | Elderly customer, sudden unusual activity (potential victim) |
| **Baseline Shift** | Customer's pattern suddenly changes dramatically |

### How to Respond in the Live Assessment

**DO:**
- ✅ Think out loud ("I notice X because...")
- ✅ Use framework ("This appears to be Layering because...")
- ✅ Reference Days 1-7 concepts (velocity, retention, etc.)
- ✅ Ask clarifying questions ("Does the customer have a history of this?")
- ✅ Prioritize risks ("Most concerning is X, then Y")
- ✅ Consider context ("Given the customer age, this could be account takeover")
- ✅ Stay calm and methodical

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Rush to judgment ("This is definitely fraud!")
- ❌ Make up facts ("I assume they're in a high-risk country")
- ❌ Be overconfident ("I'm 100% sure")
- ❌ Freeze up ("I don't know")
- ❌ Blame the customer ("They're obviously guilty")

(Is this legitimate growth? Fraud? Account misuse? Why?)

**Q3: "What would you do next as an investigator?"**
(Your investigation plan in 3-4 steps.)

**Write your responses as if you're speaking in the interview.**

1. Request documentation from "Creative Services Ltd" and "Business Growth Fund" (verify these entities are real)
2. Ask Angela why funds are being sent to personal accounts (what's the business purpose?)

**Hint:**  
Don't overthink. Look for patterns. Apply Days 4-7 concepts. Speak clearly about what you see and why it's concerning. Show your logic.
